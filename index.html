Welcome to the Child Language Lab

At the Child Language Lab, we investigate how children acquire and develop language skills. Our research combines playful exploration with rigorous scientific methods to uncover the fascinating world of child language development.

Did You Know?

Babies are born able to distinguish sounds from all languages, but by around 12 months, they specialize in the sounds of their native language, losing this universal ability.

Our Research

Our cutting-edge research focuses on various aspects of child language acquisition:

  • Child Language Acquisition: Understanding how children learn the meanings of words and syntactic structures.
  • Language Change Over Time: Investigating how language learning in childhood contributes to linguistic evolution.
  • Linguistic Modality: Focusing on expressions that indicate possibility, necessity, and likelihood (e.g., must, have to, maybe).
  • Counterfactuality: Examining language that expresses non-real situations, imagined worlds, and hypothetical scenarios (e.g., I wish Stegosauruses were still around).
  • Complex Thought Expression: Studying how language captures open possibilities, non-actual scenarios, and hypothetical or imagined worlds.

"We're particularly interested in how children acquire complex linguistic structures. Their incredible ability to learn language continues to amaze and inspire our research." - Dr. Cournane
